- Support is committed to an Artist/Band source and becomes unavailable to allocate elsewhere while committed.
- Type: settled direction; withdrawal and expiry rules remain open.
- Likely owner: future dedicated Support contract.

- A listener regains committed Support by following through with verified support for the source, preventing unsupported declarations and inflated backing counts.
- Type: settled direction; qualifying proof families and release values remain open.

## Feature Sets

- Two-part city-tier architecture program
- Raw basis: "there should be 2 parts this and the other request since we dont
  need this for it to function."
- Included behavior:
  - Part 1 delivers the functioning city-tier music/community platform without a
    dependency on Support/Participation runtime;
  - Part 2 adds Support commitments, Participation, influence tracing,
    analytics, collectible fulfillment, Trusted Roles, and future governance as
    an integrated civic layer.
- Excluded / not activated:
  - blocking core listening, source registration, community activation, Release
    Deck, RADIYO/Fair Play, Plot, or event functionality on Part 2;
  - treating Part 2's open scoring and governance decisions as launch blockers
    for Part 1.
- Status: settled program boundary; each part still needs its own scoped
  deliverables.

- Proof-backed revolving Support
- Raw basis: "100 support", "get it back by actually supporting", and "prevents inflating support numbers / gaming".
- Included behavior:
  - fixed starting allocation;
  - source-specific commitments as an optional structured path rather than a
    prerequisite for every Participation award;
  - primary fulfillment through any number of verified direct support
    opportunities/contracts issued by the committed source;
  - possible future micro-credit from influence-traced indirect outcomes;
  - request/commit and opportunity/action lifecycle terminology;
  - fixed-value direct opportunities and a still-open influence model for
    Recommendation-derived participation or Support bonuses.
  - source `Announce` actions that publish eligible dashboard signals into the
    Feed as support-directing contract surfaces.
  - upcoming-show Feed announcements as the first concrete Support Request card.
  - atomic one-unit show commitments fulfilled through verified attendance.
  - event-specific QR verification as a supported attendance-proof path.
  - successful attendance proof restores the committed unit and awards the
    event's digital flyer to the listener's collection.
  - attendance without a prior commitment can still earn Participation but does
    not mint the contract-attached flyer.
  - separate perceived/community-support, proven-support, and broader reach
    analytics.
  - multi-artist events as multi-beneficiary Support contexts independent of
    financial splits.
  - direct-supporter analytics preserved per billed artist.
  - audience-draw insight showing which billed artists motivated attendance.
  - followed-artist Announcements delivered through a direct channel, with event
    Announcements automatically entering the listener's calendar.
  - source-attached digital goodies as optional fulfillment rewards for other
    Support Request types.
  - request-type-owned default proof with optional additional opportunities.
  - action-owned proof, value, attribution, and anti-duplication behavior.
  - a multi-hop influence graph capable of linking Recommend, search, Collect,
    Blast, and later Collect actions when attribution is defensible.
  - provenance edges for establishing entities whose formation expands or
    proliferates the community, potentially including Artist/Band sources,
    Causes, Sects, Communities, and participating venues.
  - influence-based restoration or bonus Support bandwidth as the non-direct
    complement to direct contract fulfillment.
  - replenishment after proof;
  - source-visible supporter history;
  - multiple proof subsystems with explicit values.
  - Participation earned from qualifying song listening.
- Excluded / not activated:
  - cash value or payout;
  - Fair Play or tier-progression influence;
  - weighted voting;
  - automatic council seats;
  - runtime implementation before the ledger, proof, privacy, abuse, and governance contracts are settled.
- Status: design in progress.

- Support-qualified Community Council
- Raw basis: "permissions access to make decisions within a community" and "or at least a council".
- Included behavior:
  - `Participation` earned through verified supportive actions, with or without
    a preceding Support contract, may qualify listeners for bounded community
    decision-making and Trusted Roles;
  - available Support, pending commitments, purchased Runs, money spent, and
    collectible ownership do not determine eligibility by themselves.
- Excluded / not activated:
  - direct voting weight proportional to Support;
  - control of Fair Play, song ordering, or tier progression;
  - automatic authority from pending/unfulfilled Support.
- Status: open; governance model not yet selected.

- Community-owned network transition
- Raw basis: "phase out the business end" and "communities would be able to

- Included behavior:
  - Support and verified participation may become legitimacy inputs for mature

  - Community councils may become stewardship bodies for locally sustained
    network operations;
  - Central business control may reduce as communities become capable of

  - immediate decentralization;
  - token, equity, cooperative, franchise, nonprofit, treasury, or legal-entity

  - independent technical forks or incompatible local platform rules;
  - transfer of infrastructure, data, liabilities, or intellectual property

  - automatic artist payouts from Support totals;
  - an assumed funding source, treasury, legal custodian, tax treatment, or

  - council control before financial governance and conflict rules are locked.

## Working Interpretation

- Support is evolving from a simple Feed-card action into the contract layer
  beneath source announcements, listener commitments, and verified supportive
  action.
- The listener's starting `100 Support` represents scarce declared backing, but
  should not yet be described as a percentage. Committing it signals intent;
  verified action proves follow-through and replenishes the committed amount.
- The first concrete contract is intentionally atomic: one show Support click
  commits one Support unit, and verified attendance restores that unit.
- The digital flyer is the collectible result of fulfilling the prior event
  Support contract, not a reward for attendance alone and not the Support unit
  or Participation value.
- More generally, a Request may disclose an attached digital goodie before the
  listener commits; fulfillment, not commitment alone, triggers minting.
- Request templates provide their required/default proof automatically. Source
  operators should not rebuild the contract grammar for each announcement.
- A Request supplies the beneficiary and contextual object; qualifying actions
  carry their own fulfillment mechanics and apply them when attributable to that
  Request.
- `Participation` is the separate cumulative value recording verified supportive
  actions, whether or not a Request/Commit preceded them. It is the valuable
  point record used toward future role qualification; award rules remain open.
- Qualifying song listening can add directly to the Participation record without
  requiring a Request contract.
- Listening is the minimum Participation action because a person must take part
  in the music itself to be part of the community ecosystem.
- Participation value comes from verified supportive actions and their defined
  values, not merely from the amount of Support committed or held.
- An action's value only fulfills a Support contract when the action is
  attributable to that contract, directly or through an approved indirect
  evidence path.
- Contract attribution follows verified outcomes, not merely the source on which
  the action occurred if the exploratory indirect path is later activated.
  Cross-source benefit must be recorded explicitly rather than inferred from
  engagement data.
- The primary contract closes through source-issued direct support opportunities
  whose verified values add up to the listener's outstanding Support amount.
- Fractional restoration from indirect outcomes is an open design option, not a
  generic activity reward. Raw downstream clicks may contribute only a very
  small amount inside a broader influence-tracing system; stronger downstream
  outcomes such as Collect and Blast propagation provide more meaningful
  evidence. The model may apply to establishments and music signals rather than
  being limited to either one.
- The cleanest current interpretation is two related ledgers: finite revolving
  `Support` for commitments, and cumulative `Participation` earned from verified
  acts and attributable influence. Strong influence may restore Support or earn
  extra Support bandwidth. Influence can continue creating earning potential,
  but the persistence of each resulting bandwidth award is unresolved.
- `Announce` and `Recommend` are parallel publication/discovery actions around
  eligible signals, but they do not imply identical actor authority or
  fulfillment value. The source Announces; the listener Recommends; the signal's
  support contract defines what commitment and verified fulfillment mean.
- Establishment influence is broader than venue/business referrals but narrower
  than all entity creation. It includes attributable participation in creating
  entities that expand or proliferate the community and tracking the downstream
  effects that follow from their creation.
- Artists need separate visibility into pending commitments and fulfilled Support so promises cannot be mistaken for completed backing.
- Sources need separate analytics for perceived/community Support commitments
  and proven fulfilled Support. The commitment layer helps measure anticipated
  support; contract fulfillment proves that Support. Verified actions without a
  prior commitment can still contribute to broader reach and Participation
  without necessarily becoming proven Support.
- Event Support attribution follows the billed lineup rather than the event's
  private revenue split. The event can show total reach/support while each artist
  retains a direct-supporter view of the audience relationship attributable to
  that artist.
- Per-artist direct-supporter analytics exist to show audience draw, not to
  divide ticket revenue or rank artists in Fair Play.
- Current interpretation awaiting founder confirmation: one verified attendance
  action fulfills Participation once for the listener; the artist relationship
  that motivated attendance receives direct-support attribution; other billed
  artists receive secondary-support attribution. This avoids dividing or
  multiplying the listener's Participation award across the lineup.
- The civic opportunity is Trusted Role qualification through earned
  Participation, not purchasing or accumulating weighted authority.
- The longer-term opportunity is a staged path from participation, to verified
  stewardship, to community self-support and potential network ownership. The
  exact ownership object and transition mechanism are not yet defined.
- The intended economic outcome includes a sustainable source of real money for
  local live music. Support remains a social/participation system unless a
  future financial contract explicitly defines a join point.
- Print Shop is the first named spending rail for community live-music funds.
  This does not yet determine whether councils allocate money, Support converts
  into campaign credits, or another auditable mechanism selects campaigns.
- The same Print Shop and local-business rails also provide initial UPRISE
  revenue. A future transition contract must define how scene-attributed revenue
  moves from central operation toward community support or ownership.
- The broader purpose is to rebuild a self-reinforcing local broadcast and live
  music pipeline rather than operate Support as an isolated loyalty feature.
- Community funds, councils, and Support must strengthen the local ecosystem
  without purchasing or bypassing Fair Play progression. National attention
  remains an outcome of the broadcast path, not a council-awarded placement.
- Limited-run minting is a central economic and evidentiary rail: it can generate
  revenue, produce collectible proof, and connect real participation back to the
  listener/source relationship without becoming speculative property.

## Do Not Drift

- Do not treat pending Support as fulfilled support.
- Do not use starting/available Support, pending allocations, money spent, or
  collectible ownership as the role-qualification measure. Participation earned
  through verified supportive activity is the value that can satisfy role gates,
  even when no Request/Commit preceded the action.
- Do not let one listener allocate the same Support units to multiple sources simultaneously.
- Do not replenish Support from trivial repeatable clicks.
- Do not let unrelated activity, even when valuable elsewhere, fulfill a Support
  contract without direct or approved indirect attribution to that contract.
- Do not assume that an action involving one source benefits another source;
  cross-source fulfillment requires explicit causal evidence and must prevent
  accidental double counting.
- Do not convert Support into Fair Play, ranking, tier progression, or weighted voting.
- Do not require listeners to complete a mandated activity category, sequence,
  purchase, attendance pattern, or service checklist before they can qualify
  through the general action-value system.
- Do not treat the council idea as approved governance runtime yet.
- Do not infer cash value, payout, redemption, or transferability between listeners and sources before those rules are explicitly settled.
- Do not treat long-term community ownership as permission to invent tokens,
  equity, treasuries, franchises, legal entities, or independent infrastructure.
- Do not equate Support units with dollars or promise artist payouts before the
  community-fund source, custody, eligibility, and allocation rules are settled.
- Do not let community-funded Print Shop marketing alter Fair Play rotation,
  voting, tier progression, or civic authority.
- Do not add resale, exchange markets, bidding, speculation, cash-out, or open
  transfer mechanics to the closed digital economy.
- Do not use coin, token, wallet, or spend as user-facing language unless a
  later naming decision explicitly approves it.
- Do not use agreement/debt-contract terminology as the preferred product
  language after the Request/Commit/Fulfill/Act correction.
- Do not treat source Feed actions as ordinary social posting. Source
  announcements exist to gather or direct support around eligible signals and
  their contracts.
- Do not treat a raw downstream Recommendation click as direct fulfillment of a
  Support commitment or award it uncapped value. Any micro-credit requires the
  approved influence graph, attribution rules, deduplication, and abuse limits.
- Do not interpret `establishment` here as only a venue or local business. The
  primary meaning is establishing a qualifying community-expanding entity
  through approved Registrar/community lifecycles.
- Do not award establishment influence merely because a record or account was
  created; the entity must belong to the approved expansion/proliferation class.
- Do not limit influence tracing to music recommendations; the eventual contract
  should be systemic across eligible actions and entity-establishment events.
- Do not collapse finite Support commitment capacity and cumulative
  Participation/Influence evidence into one number before their different
  lifecycle and authority roles are settled.
- Do not treat raw play starts, loops, muted/background farming, or unlimited
  repeats as qualifying Participation until listening evidence and anti-abuse
  rules are defined.
- Do not describe listening as passive or valueless within the Participation
  system; it is the foundational form of taking part.
- Do not make the functioning city-tier music platform depend on completion of
  the Support/Participation civic layer.
- Do not grant influence-based Support bandwidth from unverified propagation or
  before growth rates, ceilings, attribution, and anti-collusion rules are
  defined.
- Do not describe earned Support bandwidth as permanent, temporary, expiring,
  renewable, or uncapped until that lifecycle decision is settled.
- Do not add an amount picker to the atomic upcoming-show Support Request or let
  non-attendance activity fulfill its Proof-of-Attendance contract.
- Do not accept replayed, cross-event, or unauthenticated QR scans as attendance
  proof.
- Do not mint an attached Request goodie before the contract is fulfilled.
- Do not require a Request/Commit before every Participation award.
- Do not mint a contract-attached event flyer for attendance that was never
  preceded by the required event Support commitment.
- Do not collapse perceived/community Support, proven Support, and broader reach
  analytics into one number.
- Do not allocate event Support according to artist payment splits; financial
  settlement and Support attribution are separate systems.
- Do not use per-artist event draw analytics to alter Fair Play, tier progression,
  or the event's private payment split.
- Do not multiply the listener's Participation award merely because one event
  has multiple billed artists; multi-beneficiary source analytics and listener
  Participation accounting require separate rules.
