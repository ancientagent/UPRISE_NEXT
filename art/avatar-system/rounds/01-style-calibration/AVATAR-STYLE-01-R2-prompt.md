# AVATAR-STYLE-01 R2 Prompt Packet

Status: generation brief
Date: 2026-07-22

## Shared Content Lock

Each direction is one clean 1536 x 1024 character sheet with eight original,
front-facing, belly-up avatars. The sheet is not UI, a collage of styles, or a
production parts export.

Across the eight avatars, communicate these music-community contexts through
restrained clothing and styling rather than labels or costumes:

- punk;
- hip-hop;
- country/folk;
- metal/hardcore;
- electronic/dance;
- poetry/spoken word;
- jazz/instrumental;
- indie/alternative.

Requirements:

- different skull, jaw, nose, eye, mouth, ear, and shoulder constructions;
- six respectful skin tones distributed across the set;
- visible shirts, jackets, vests, suspenders, hats, and patch/button surfaces;
- shared neck-socket logic and three reusable shoulder/body families;
- separate-hair and headwear compatibility must remain plausible;
- flexible identity cues without demographic caricature;
- no photorealism, 3D, anime, chibi, mascots, celebrity likenesses, readable
  writing, logos, circles, portrait cards, scenery, or decorative frames;
- warm off-white background with black, neutral color, and restrained
  fluorescent UPRISE accents;
- the prior avatar boards are not visual-style references.

## Direction A - Record-Shop Editorial Ink

Hand-authored brush and technical-pen drawing with distinctive facial geometry,
economical hatch, flat screenprinted color, slight analog ink spread, and strong
small-size silhouettes. Nostalgic independent-music editorial character art,
not a photocopy filter or generic punk clip art.

## Direction B - Retro Cel Comic

Crisp hand-drawn cel-animation construction with confident contour variation,
flat color, understated facial acting, and tactile painted fills. Warm late-20th-
century independent animation energy without anime, children's-TV proportions,
or glossy modern game art.

## Direction C - Carved Ink and Gouache

Chunky linocut-like contour mixed with irregular gouache color shapes, selective
dry-brush texture, and highly differentiated head silhouettes. Graphic and
tactile but with enough facial information for users to recognize themselves;
not featureless icons or posterized photographs.

## Review Rule

R2 is approved, revised, or rejected by direction. No direction becomes the
base until the founder explicitly approves it.
