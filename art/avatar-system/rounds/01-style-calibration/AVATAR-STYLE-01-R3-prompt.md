# AVATAR-STYLE-01 R3 Prompt Packet

Status: generation brief
Date: 2026-07-22

## Correction Lock

R3 treats the avatar as a stylized paper-doll fit model, not a portrait. Faces
use a deliberately small mark vocabulary: simple eyes, one nose mark, one mouth
mark, optional brow marks, and simplified ears. Do not draw detailed lips,
nostrils, cheekbones, eyelashes, wrinkles, realistic facial hair, or modeled
anatomy shading.

Eight belly-up figures demonstrate punk, hip-hop, country/folk,
metal/hardcore, electronic/dance, poetry/spoken word, jazz/instrumental, and
indie/alternative contexts. Community expression comes from interchangeable
hair, clothing, headwear, and accessories rather than fixed facial identity or
demographic caricature.

The presentation is monochrome: black, off-white, and grayscale, with one
fluorescent acid-chartreuse accent used sparingly on clothing, hair, or optional
accessories. Skin uses six tonal values for this style test rather than realistic
portrait coloring. The accent is not used as a skin tone.

The shared system constraints from R2 remain active: combined head-face presets,
separate hair, a shared neck socket, three body bases, six skin tones, visible
merch surfaces, and no text or logos.

## Direction A - Rubber-Stamp Geometry

Chunky hand-cut geometric head shapes, blunt black contour, minimal face marks,
flat spot colors, and controlled rubber-stamp edge variation. Bold and mature,
not clip art or emoji.

## Direction B - Loose Marker Figures

Expressive felt-marker contour, uneven but controlled line rhythm, sparse flat
color blocks, slightly oversized heads, and intentionally incomplete facial
marks. Friendly underground-comic energy without portrait detail.

## Direction C - Paper-Cut Animation

Layered cut-paper shapes with restrained ink outlines, subtle offset-print
registration, simplified face pieces, and strong swappable silhouettes. Tactile
and graphic without becoming featureless symbols or children's craft art.

## Review Rule

No R3 direction becomes the base until explicitly approved by the founder.
