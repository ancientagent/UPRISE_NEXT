# AVATAR-STYLE-01 R4 Prompt Packet

Status: generation brief
Date: 2026-07-22

## Shared Lock

R4 refines R3 direction 3 toward cohesive illustrated sticker art. The two
supplied example sheets are mood references only. Use their bold silhouette,
simple face, varied hair, graphic ink, and die-cut energy without copying their
people, repeated face construction, clothing, skulls, text, or punk-only lineup.

Each sheet shows eight heavily stylized, facially ambiguous, belly-up avatars
covering punk, hip-hop, country/folk, metal/hardcore, electronic/dance,
poetry/spoken word, jazz/instrumental, and indie/alternative contexts.

Requirements:

- strong variation in head silhouettes, jaw geometry, eye/nose/mouth marks,
  shoulder builds, hair mass, and headwear;
- minimal reusable face marks with no portrait anatomy;
- black, off-white, and grayscale with one fluorescent acid-chartreuse accent;
- a bold die-cut sticker edge around each complete figure;
- cohesive illustration rather than visible paper-doll seams;
- simple shared neck registration remains plausible underneath the illustration;
- every jacket, vest, cardigan, overshirt, and other outerwear layer stays open;
- the undershirt chest plane remains unobstructed, blank, and readable for
  future band shirts, printing, patches, or digital merchandise;
- community character comes through interchangeable styling rather than fixed
  demographic facial types;
- no readable text, logos, circular portraits, cards, scenery, or UI.

## Direction A - Die-Cut Ink Sticker

Bold clean hand-inked contour, heavy black masses, simple interior marks, crisp
white kiss-cut border, and limited acid-chartreuse highlights. Closest to the
reference's sticker energy but with substantially more construction diversity.

## Direction B - Screenprinted Comic Sticker

More expressive brush illustration, varied line weight, selective dry-brush and
halftone texture, flat monochrome fills, fluorescent spot accent, and a slightly
irregular white sticker border. Authored and lively without portrait detail.

## Review Rule

R4 contains two experiments so the active review remains lightweight. No R4
direction becomes the base until explicitly approved by the founder.
