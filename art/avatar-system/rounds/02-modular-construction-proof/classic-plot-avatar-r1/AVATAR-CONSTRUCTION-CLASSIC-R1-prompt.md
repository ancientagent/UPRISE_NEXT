## Inherited Locks

- Classic Plot/Home Scene black-ink illustrated character language.
- Heavily stylized, facially ambiguous comic identities; no portrait realism.
- Belly-up crop with clothing and shirt graphics readable at Feed-rail size.
- Shared neck socket; heads, hair, bodies, tops, outerwear, and details remain
  visually separable.
- Monochrome black, off-white, and grayscale with one restrained fluorescent
  yellow-green accent outside skin.
- All jackets, vests, overshirts, and suspenders remain open so the undershirt
  chest plane is visible.
- Neutral asset IDs and body/head shapes; no gendered or demographic labels.

## Proof Sheet

Create one square, clean visual construction sheet at 2048 x 2048. This is an
art-system proof, not a UI screen, phone mockup, wardrobe catalog, or moodboard.

Show:

1. Three neutral belly-up body bases: narrow, standard, and broad shoulders.
   Every body uses the exact same centered neck socket, shoulder line, and
   belly crop.
2. Three clearly different head-face presets with minimal eye, nose, and mouth
   marks. Heads must vary in width, jaw, chin, cheek, ear, and brow geometry.
3. Five hair states as separate silhouettes: bald, short crop, shag, upright
   spikes, and textured/loc silhouette. Hair must not be permanently attached
   to a head.
4. Two open starter clothing assemblies: plain tee and readable graphic tee.
5. One open jacket or vest and one suspender overlay, each exposing the tee.
6. One plain beanie demonstrating a clear hair-compatibility boundary.
7. One small nose/ear piercing detail, one neck detail, and one separate button
   attached to an explicitly shown lapel or suspender zone.
8. Three assembled avatars using swapped parts to prove that the same head,
   body, hair, and clothing language remains cohesive.
9. One compact Feed-rail preview and one larger Home/Plot-shell preview of the
   same assembled avatar.

Use flat front-facing registration, consistent scale, clean spacing, no
perspective, no dynamic poses, and no decorative background beyond a plain
off-white sheet. Use short neutral labels only where they clarify asset
families. Do not add product UI, stats, controls, speech bubbles, source names,
or invented features.

## Rejection Conditions

- one repeated face with only different hair;
- photorealistic anatomy or portrait-level specificity;
- circular avatar crops;
- closed jackets or hidden chest-print area;
- baked-in hair, headwear, buttons, patches, or skin color;
- gendered labels or scene stereotypes;
- production-ready claim, transparent-part claim, or editable-layer claim;
- new runtime, editor, marketplace, collectible, or social behavior.
